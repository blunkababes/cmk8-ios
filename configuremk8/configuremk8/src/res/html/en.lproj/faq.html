What do all of the attributes mean?

Acceleration
The rate that it takes for a vehicle to reach its top speed. If you find yourself crashing or going off road a lot, then this attribute might be important for you.

Average Speed
The averaged top speed of the vehicle. Does not affect off-road travel. The average speed is equally weighted from the following 4 attributes:
  • Ground Speed: The top speed of the vehicle on land with normal gravity.
  • Anti-Gravity Speed: The top speed of the vehicle in anti-gravity mode
  • Air Speed: The top speed of the vehicle while gliding.
  • Water Speed: The top speed of the vehicle while driving underwater.

Average Handling
The averaged turning ability of the vehicle. The higher the stat, the sharper the vehicle can turn. The average handling is equally weighted from the following 4 attributes:
  • Ground Handling: The turning ability of the vehicle on land with normal gravity.
  • Anti-Gravity Handling: The turning ability of the vehicle in anti-gravity mode
  • Air Handling: The turning ability of the vehicle while gliding.
  • Water Handling: The turning ability of the vehicle while driving underwater.

Miniturbo
The strength of the vehicle's miniturbo speed boosts.

Traction
The grasp of the vehicle. Vehicles with higher traction slip less on certain terrain and can stabilize on the road better.

Weight
The weight of the vehicle. Vehicles with higher weight knock away vehicles with lower weight.

A Note About Acceleration

Although the acceleration stats can have decimal values, when actually applied they are rounded down to the nearest integer. For example, a configuration with acceleration of 4.25 has the same acceleration as a configuration with an acceleration of 4.75. They are both rounded to 4. The calculator for creating custom configurations takes this into account when scoring each combination. However, the rounded value is not shown to the user when viewing stats.

How are the custom configurations calculated?

The custom configurations are calculated by taking a weighted average of the following 6 attributes: Acceleration, Average Speed, Average Handling, Miniturbo, Traction, and Weight. Each attribute has an equal influence on the total score. Note: It may be hard to come up with a variety of configurations since the game does not seem to favor the more balanced characters.

Stats Not Shown

Body frame sizes
The size of the frame can influence how large a target the vehicle is for things like shells and being hit by other players. The 3 sizes available are influenced only by character group (weight class):
  • Small: Flyweights and Featherweights
  • Medium: Lightweights and Middleweights
  • Large: Cruiserweights, Metalweights, Heavyweights

Drift
This affects how the vehicle slides when power sliding. There are 2 types: outward drift and inward drift. Outward drift forces the vehicle to slide outwards, more like a real drift. Inward drift allows the vehicle to take sharp turns while drifting. All vehicles have outward drift, except for Vehicle Group F which contains the 4 bikes: Comet, Sport Bike, Jet Bike, and Yoshi Bike. These bikes have inward drift.

What about My Mii?

The Mii will take 1 of the 3 character groups (weight classes), depending on the Mii's size that you configured in the Mii Maker.
  • Small -> Flyweight
  • Medium -> Middleweight
  • Large -> Heavyweight
